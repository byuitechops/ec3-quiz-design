Use common greetings and ‘get-to-know-you’ questions and responses

CAN-DO: Can understand and exchange greetings

Learn

You greet someone when you first meet. Greet friends informally. Great others formally. Make eye contact. It is also common to shake hands and smile. You can also ask questions and answer questions to get to know the other person better.

When you meet someone new, you should do the following:

  • Greet someone.
  • State your name.
  • State another’s name (if you are with another person)
  • State another’s position or relationship.
  • Express pleasure at meeting someone.
  • Respond to cordiality.

Vocabulary

  • Greet: a polite welcome
  • Informal: casual
  • Formal: proper
  • Pleasure: Joy
  • Cordiality: Welcoming

Study

Greet someone.

  • Good morning (afternoon or evening)

State your name.

  • My name is _____.

State another’s name.

  • This is _____.

State another’s position or relationship.

  • She/He is _____.
  • This is _____.
    • my friend
    • my wife
  • These are my _____.
    • Parents
    • children

Greet someone.

  • How do you do?
  • My name is ____.

Respond to cordiality

  • It’s good to meet you too.

Express pleasure at meeting someone.

  • It’s good to meet you.
  • It’s a pleasure.

Talk about your home

  • Where do you come from?
  • I’m from _____.

Ask about occupation.

  • What do you do?
  • What does he/she do?
  • Do you work?
  • Does she/he work?
