Hypothetical Scenarios

GOAL: Understand hypothetical situations

Learn

“Hypothetical” describes something that is not real, but is possible. For example, it is possible to eat a taco tomorrow. It is not sure that you will eat a taco tomorrow. But it is possible. This is a hypothetical situation. We use conditional verb tenses for hypothetical situations.


    Some hypothetical situations will probably or likely be true if they happen. It is not sure that they will be true, but they probably will be. For example, if you rest, you will feel better. This is hypothetical because you are not resting right now, so it is not real. But you will probably or likely feel better if you rest. For hypothetical situations that are probably or likely true, use simple present tense and future tense.


    Hypothetical, probably or likely true:


    If + subject + verb + Object, + then + subject + will + verb + object.

    If you work on your goal, then you will achieve it.

    Vocabulary

    Hypothetical: something that is not real, but is possible.

    Situation: a set of things that are happening or conditions that exist.

    Conditional: something must be true for the second thing to be true.

    Likely: probably will happen. Almost sure that it will happen.

    Probably: likely to happen. Almost sure that it will happen.

Study

Hypothetical situations that are probably or likely true:

If she visits tomorrow, I will feel happy.

If you serve others, you will find joy.

If you teach your children righteousness, they will be righteous.

Practice

Talk to a friend. Tell your friend about hypothetical situations that are probably or likely true in your life.

Possible Answers

If I work hard, I will be successful.

If I don’t exercise, I will feel tired.

If I talk to many people, I will make more friends.
