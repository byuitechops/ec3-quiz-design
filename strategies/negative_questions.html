Complex Questions

GOAL: Learn to make Negative Questions

Learn

English has many ways to ask questions. Some are simple and some are complicated.


One complicated question type is negative questions.Negative questions do not say negative things. They use negative phrases in them (not, don’t, aren’t). For example, “Don’t you want to go?” This question does not ask something negative. It asks if you want to go. But it uses the negative phrase “don’t.” So it is a negative question.

Negative questions are used for two reasons:

  1. To confirm something you think is true. For example, I think you want to eat pizza. But I am not sure. Can i ask you "Don't you want to eat pizza?"
  2. To express opinions politely. For example, I thin kpizza is better than spaghettie. But you think is spaghetti is better than pizza. I want to be polite. So I say "Isn't pizza better than spaghetti?"

    3. Form negative questions in this way:

    Auxillary + n't + subject + verb + object

    Are n't you going home?

    Vocabulary

    Confirm: to become sure that something you believe is true is actually true

    Negative: in grammar, negative means words that have "not" in them. For example, doesn't isn't, hasn't, won't, etc

    Polite: respectful actions and manners

    Auxillary: words that add to the meaning of verbs. For example, can, will, do

    Subject: person/thing creating the action in a sentence

    Verb: the action in a sentence

    Object: person/thing receiving the action in a sentence

Study

  • Wouldn't you like a drink?
  • Why don't you come to my house?
  • Hasn't he finished studying?
  • Wouldn't it be good to start studying now?
  • Why doesn't she stop working?
  • Didn't you eat pizza yesterday?

Practice

Talk with a friend. Ask your friend negative questions about his/her life.
