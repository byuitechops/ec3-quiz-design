Understand or Express Agreement and Disagreement

CAN-DO: Can understand and express (dis)agreementt

Learn

Sometimes, we think other people’s ideas are true. Sometimes, we think other people’s ideas are not true. When we think other people’s ideas are true, we agree with their ideas. When we think other people’s ideas are not true, we disagree with their ideas.


For example, your friend says that chocolate tastes bad. You do not think this is true. You think chocolate tastes good. This means you disagree with your friend. But if you also think chocolate tastes bad, then you agree with your friend.

Vocabulary

Agree:  When we think the things someone says are true

Disagree: When we think the things someone says are not true

Study

Expressing agreement and disagreement:


Agreement

I agree with you/him.

I share your view.

I think so.

You are/he is right.

It is true.

That is right.

Disagreement

I don't agree.

I disagree.

I don't think so.

You are/he is wrong.

I don't think that's right.

I don't agree with you/him.

Practice

Tell your friend if you agree or disagree with these sentences:

Boys and girls should not play sports together.

Families are important today.

Video games are good for children.

Answers

I don't agree that boys and girls should not play sports together.

It is true that families are important today.

I disagree. Video games are not good for children.
