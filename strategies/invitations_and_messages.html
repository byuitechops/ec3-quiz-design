Invitations and Messages

GOAL: Understand and make invitations

Learn

Almost every day people ask you to do things. Sometimes they ask you to help them. This is a request. Sometimes they just want to do something with you (eat food, go to a film, go to the park, etc.). This is an invitation. To make these invitations, people usually use simple written messages. It is important to know what these simple messages look like.

Vocabulary

Request: the act of asking for something

Invitation: a request to do something

Message: written or spoken information that is given or sent to someone

Study

Voice Message

Hi Sara, This is Maria. I am going to a party tomorrow night at 7. It’s at the church. Would you like to come to the party? Please call me back and let me know.


In this example, Maria is giving an invitation to Sara. She is asking Sara to go to the movies with her. Notice that her invitation is in the form of a question: “Would you like to come to the party?”

Text Message

Holly: Hey Jen, I’m going to the movies with Clint tonight. Do you want to come?

Jenny: I wish I could. But I can’t. I’ve got to help my mom with some things.

Holly: That’s too bad. Let us know if something changes.


In this example, Holly is giving an invitation to Jenny. She is asking Jenny to go to the movies with her. Notice that her invitation is in the form of a question: “Do you want to come?”

Invitation Examples:

Can you come to the part?

Would you come to the party?

Will come to the party?

Practice

Read the text message. Find the invitation. Only one of the questions is an invitation.

Matt: Hi, this is Josh Garret. I'd like to make an appointment to see Dr. Smith

Paula: What is the appointment for?

Matt: I'm having trouble with my shoulder. Do you have an opening today?

Paula: No, I'm sorry. The earliest available time is tomorrow at 3:00.

Write an invitation. You want to go to the movies with your friend Sam.Text a message about the movie. Make sure to include the DATE, TIME, and MOVIE. Ask him to text you back.

Listen Your friend left you this message: Hello, This is Jon. Star Wars is in the theater tomorrow at 7. Would you come to the movie with me? It will be fun. Please call me back and let me know.

Speak: Call Jon and give a response.

Answers

Read: Do you want to get something to eat with me?

Write:

You: Hi Sam. I am going to a ward party tonight. It’s at the church at 7. Do you want to come? You can bring some chips.

Sam: Yes. That sounds fun!

Hi Jon. Yes, I can go to the movies with you. Sounds fun!
