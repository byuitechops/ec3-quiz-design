Capitalization

GOAL: Understand Proper Capitalization Rules

Learn

Some words in English use a capital letter, also called the upper case, for the initial letter. This lesson contains the most common capitalization rules.


Capitalize the words below.

  1. Names and proper nouns (countries, cities, nationalities, languages, events, days, months, holidays, specific places, buildings)
  2. The first word of a sentence
  3. The pronoun I
  4. Most of the words in titles
  5. The first word of a quote

Vocabulary

Nationality: Belonging to a particular nation

Proper noun: The name of a person, place, or thing

Quote: To repeat the exact words used by another person

Study

Names and proper nouns

  • My favorite author is JK Rowling.
  • I will call Maria and Elizabeth.
  • We attended a Catholic wedding in New York City last year.

The first word of a sentence

  • Correct: The dog is sleeping.
  • Incorrect: the dog is sleeping.

The pronoun "I"

  • Correct: I can bring you a book.
  • Incorrect: i can bring you a book.

Titles

  • JK Rowling wrote Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone.

Quotes

  • Maya Angelou said, "Try to be a rainbow in someone's cloud".

Practice

Capitalize the words in the following sentences correctly:

  • mrs. garcia is my favorite teacher.
  • Can you give jake a call?
  • Please tell me when i can help you.
  • My favorite book is one hundred years of solitude by gabriela garcia marquez.
  • President Uchtdorf said, “he created us to have joy!”

Answers

  • Mrs. Garcia is my favorite teacher.
  • Can you give Jake a call?
  • Please tell me when I can help you.
  • My favorite book is One Hundred Years of Solitude by Gabriela Garcia Marquez.
  • President Uchtdorf said, “He created us to have joy!”
