Meaning of Words Based on Context

GOAL:Use context to understand new words.

Learn

You will find new words as you read and listen. Do not stop to translate every new word you find. Good language learners focus on the information that they understand, and use it to make an inference (a conclusion or a guess) about the meaning of new words.

Vocabulary

Inference: a guess or conclusion, using information you understand and know to help you understand information you don’t know.

Context: information around a new word.

Study

Jen: My kidsmisbehavedtoday, Jane. They made a big mess, they broke the window, and they were unkind to their grandma!


Jane:I am so sorry!

Jane does not know what “misbehaved” means. But Jane understands that making messes, breaking things, and being unkind are bad things to do. She makes an inference, and concludes that “misbehaved” means doing bad things.

Practice

Guess the meaning of the bolded words. Do not translate the words. Use the words you know to guess the meaning of the bolded words.

      John was so hungry. He ate all the food. He didn’t leave any crumbson his plate.

    (Crumb: small piece of food.)

      John loves animals. He looks at pictures of animals. He reads information about animals. John thinks animals are fascinating.

    (Fascinating: very interesting.)

      Mary eats pizza every day. John does not like pizza. John rarely eats pizza.

    (Rarely: not very often.)
