Understand commands and warnings

CAN-DO: Can ask and respond to commands and warningss

Learn

We make commands when we tell someone what to do. Warnings are a type of command. We give warnings to tell someone what to do to avoid having something bad happen.


Form commands and warnings this way:

Strong commands/warnings:

Verb Base + Object

     Shut       the door!

Polite commands:

Please + Verb Base + Object

 Please   shut       the door!

Vocabulary

Command: To give an order to tell someone to do something

Warning: Something said or written to tell people of a possible danger, problem, or other unpleasant thing that might happen

Polite: Respectful actions

Study

Strong Commands/Warnings

Stop that!

Don’t touch that! It’s hot.

Eat your food!

Do your homework!

Polite Commands

Please help me.

Please don’t do that.

Please come here.

Practice

Talk to a friend. Practice giving commands and warnings to your friend.


Possible commands and warnings to give.

Walk to the table.

Open the peanut butter.

Pick up the knife.

Do not cut yourself!  

Put peanut butter on the bread.

Eat the bread.
