COMMAS

GOAL: Correctly use commas

Learn

A comma (,) is a punctuation mark. It helps join or separate ideas in one sentence. This lesson will explain three common ways to use commas.

  • Use a comma in a list of two or more nouns
  • Use a comma to join two independent clauses when they are joined by "and, but, for, or, nor, so, yet."
  • Use a comma after an introductory phrase or adverb.

Avoid using run-on sentences (sentences that are too long) or comma splices (commas where a period should be). Use a period instead of a comma if there are two independent clauses.

Vocabulary

Independent clause: a group of words that can stand alone as a sentence. It has a subject and verb and expresses a complete thought.

Phrase: a group of words that expresses an idea but isn't a complete sentence

Run-on sentence: sentences that are too long

Comma splice: commas where a period should be

Study

Lists

Please pick up cereal, bread, milk, and butter at the store.

We all want happiness, love, and a better job.

Join independent clauses

I wanted to see the movie, but it was too expensive.

I need to go to the store, so I will be late.

Introductory phrases or adverbs

After you leave work, be sure to stop at the store.

Fortunately, I remembered to buy bread.

Run-on sentences and comma splices


Incorrect

I love to attend church, I go there often.

I went to the store they were out of milk.

Correct

I love church, and I go there often.

I love church. I go there often.

I went to the store, but they were out of milk.

I went to the store. They were out of milk.

Practice

Correct the following sentences:

I like to play soccer tennis volleyball and basketball.

He remembered to bring plates he forgot cups.

Suprisingly the weather was warm.

Before you leave please remember your lunch.

The dog chased the car he was fast he was almost hit.

Answers

I like to play soccer, tennis, volleyball, and basketball.

He remembered to bring plates, but he forgot cups.

Surprisingly, the weather was warm.

Before you leave, please remember your lunch.

The dog chased the car. He was fast. He was almost hit.
