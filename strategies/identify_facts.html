IDENTIFY FACTS

GOAL: Identify Facts

Learn

A fact is a bit of information, which is presented as truth or evidence. A fact is either true. You can prove facts. When you read or listen, it is helpful to know when someone is presenting a fact or presenting an opinion. It is also important when you speak or write to know how to use facts and opinions effectively.

Vocabulary

Opinion: a view or judgment formed about something, not necessarily based on fact or knowledge.

Prove: remove doubts or make certain.

Argue: give reasons to support a fact or opinion

Thought: an idea produced by thinking

Feeling: an emotional reaction

Idea: a thought or suggestion

Study

Facts

  • There are 1.6 kilometers in a mile.
  • Panda bears live in China.
  • Five books are on the table.
