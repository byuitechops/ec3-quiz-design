Offers

GOAL:Make offers

Learn

Make an offer by asking someone if you can help. Most of the time offers are written or spoken as questions with the following phrases:

  • Can I
  • Shall I
  • Would you like

Sometimes they are simple statements. Many times these statements are responses to general requests.

  • I will do the cleaning.
  • I can take you to school.

Vocabulary

  • Request: to ask for something
  • Offer: to present for acceptance

Study

Look at the group text below for an example.

  • To: Kenneth, Jenna, Jack, Frank
  • Liz: Hey, I’m planning Tracy’s birthday party. Can any of you bring anything?
  • Kenneth: Sure thing. I’ve got drinks.
  • Jenna: Totally.I’ll bring cake.
  • Frank:Count me in for the music
  • Jack: I won’t be able to make it. I’ve got a meeting that day.

In this example, Liz makes a general request for Tracy’s birthday party: “Can any of you bring anything?” In response to her request, there are three different offers: 1.) I’ve got drinks, 2.) I’ll bring cake, and 3.) Count me in for music.Notice that all of these offers to help are simple statements, not questions.

Practice

Readthe text messages below and find the offers. They are all written as statements.

  • To: Marc, Carey, Chris.
  • Heather: Mom is really sick, so I thought we could do a little more today to help around the house. What can you guys do to help out?
  • Chris: I’m sorry to hear she’s sick. I’ll cook dinner.
  • Marc: What does she have? I should have some time to walk the dog.
  • Heather: I think she’s just got a touch of the flu.
  • Carey: I’d be willing to clean the dishes after dinner.
  • Heather: Great! Thanks for your help guys! Mom will appreciate it.
