COMPLEX QUESTIONS

GOAL: Learn to make questions with "or"

Learn

English has many ways to ask questions. Some are simple and some are complicated.


One complicated question type is questions with “or”. We use “or” in questions when there are two options, and we need to choose one option.


For example, you can eat pizza. You can also eat spaghetti. But I don’t know what you want to eat. So I ask you: “Do you want to eat pizza or spaghetti?”


Form questions with "or" in this way:

Auxillary + subject + verb + object + or + object

Do                 you        like      baseball   or   soccer?

Will               he       study    at home    or    at school?

Vocabulary

Option: things that we can choose.

Auxillary: words that add to the meaning of verbs. For example, can, will, do

Subject: person/thing creating the action in a sentence

Verb: the action in a sentence.

Object: person/thing receiving the action in a sentence.

Study

  • Does she like dancing or singing?
  • Should you study now or later?
  • Can we go shopping today or tomorrow?

Practice

Talk with a friend. Ask your friend questions with “or” about his/her life.

Possible answers

- Do you like scary movies or romantic movies?

- Will you work today or tomorrow?

- Can you make cookies or cake?
