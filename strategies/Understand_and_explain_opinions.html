Understand and explain opinions

CAN-DO: Can discern and discuss facts/opinions and needs/wants

Learn

Opinions are what we believe or how we feel about something. Opinions are not facts. Facts are always true. When we say our opinion, we tell others what we think.


For example, if you think bananas taste good, you can say:

"I think bananas taste good."

This is your opinion. It is not a fact because other people can disagree with you. Other people can say that they think bananas taste bad. Their opinion is different from yours.

Vocabulary

  • Fact: something that is always true
  • Opinion: how you feel or what you think about something

Practice

Talk to a friend. Explain your opnion about:

  • Living in a big city
  • Having many children.
  • Eating fast foods.
  • Meeting new people.
Possible opinions:

I think that living in a big city is fun.

I believe that having many children is good for your family.

In my opinion, eating fast food is not good for you.

I prefer to meet new people because I can practice my English with them.
