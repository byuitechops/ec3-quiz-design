Strategies

Agreement and Disagreement

GOAL: Learn How to Agree and Disagree in a Conversation

Learn

Sometimes we agree with people and sometimes we disagree. It is alright to disagree, but we should be polite. Also, the way we say something-the tone of our voice-shows how we feel. We can also disagree with someone in writing - this is called argumentative or persuasive writing. We often express opinions in both writing and speaking.

Study

The phrases below are used to agree and disagree with people in conversations. Some of these phrases are more direct (and less polite) than others.


To show agreement

  • I agree with you.
  • I couldn't agree with you more.
  • That's so true.
  • So do I.
  • That's for sure.
  • Yes, I agree.
  • Tell me about it! (informal)
  • You're absolutely right.
  • That's exactly how I feel.
  • I couldn’t agree more.
  • I'm afraid I agree with ….
  • No doubt about it.
  • Me neither. (agreement with a negative statement)
  • You have a point.

To show disagreement

  • I’m not sure I agree with that.
  • I disagree.
  • I don't think so.
  • No way. (informal; direct)
  • I'm afraid I have to disagree.
  • I totally disagree. (direct)
  • I beg to differ. (direct)
  • I'd say the exactly the opposite. (direct)
  • Not necessarily.
  • That's not entirely true.
  • That's not always the case.
  • I'm not so sure about that.

To interrupt

  • Can I add something?
  • Is it okay if I jump in?
  • If I might add something...
  • Can I throw my two cents in?
  • Sorry to interrupt, but...
  • Sorry, go ahead. 
  • You were saying...
  • You didn't let me finish.

To end an argument

(when you can’t agree)
  • Let's forget about it.
  • Let's drop it.
  • I think we're going to have to agree to disagree.
  • Whatever you say. (direct)

Practice

Practice agreeing and disagreeing with friends or family members about the following topics.

The best way to travel.

The best way to learn a language.

How to find the best sale prices.

What to pack and how much to pack when going on a trip.

Who to vote for in the next election.

Which political party is the best.

The best way to look for a job.
