Requests

GOAL: Make Requests

Learn

Requests are questions used to ask others to do things. Requests usually begin with one of the following word groups: Will you, Would you, Can you, Could you.

  • Charlie, will you go to the store to buy some food?

This is a question that asks Charlie to go to the store to buy food.

The politest requests usually begin with would and could. These requests are more polite than requests that begin with will and can.

Vocabulary

Polite: showing careful thought

Study

Can you make dinner?

Could you pick me up from school?

Would you help me find my keys?

Will you wash the dishes?

Practice

Readthe requests from the list below. Choose the correct end mark for each sentence.

  • Clean your room
  • Walk the dog
  • Can you pick me up
  • Could you bring food
  • Take out the trash
  • Would you help me
  • Will you was the car
  • Eat your vegetables

Write a text message to a friend or family member and ask him or her to pick up some bread. Ask him or her to bring it to you.

Listen to a request and respond in a complete thought or sentence.

Speak your text message aloud to a friend.
