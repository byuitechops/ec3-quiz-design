Handle complaints

GOAL: Make complaints

Learn

Report a problem when you want someone to fix something. Try to be polite and apologize even though it is not your fault. You can get the attention of someone by saying “excuse me” or “sorry.” Usually, the person will work to fix the problem. Be prepared with information about the problem. This includes dates, numbers, and names of people you have talked to.


Reasons for complaints include:

  • Incorrect orders at restaurants
  • Problems with bills
  • Problems with a hotel room
  • Problems with an apartment or home rental

Vocabulary

Complaint: to express that you are not happy with something

Bill: an amount of money owed

Study

In a restaurant

  • Sorry, I think you gave me the wrong meal.
  • I’m afraid I have to make a complaint.
  • Excuse me. I think there has been a mistake.

In a hotel

  • Excuse me, but I think there is something wrong with the Internet.

To an apartment manager

  • Hello, I’m calling because I think there is something wrong with my water.

To a service representative for an online store.

  • Hello, I ordered a shirt two weeks ago. I think something went wrong. It did not arrive. Can you help me?

At a bank

  • Hello, I’m calling because I think there is a problem with my account. I was charged for something I didn’t buy.

Responses

  • I’m sorry sir/ma'am. I will check on it for you right now.
  • I apologize. I will take care of this.

Practice

Read the conversation. Find the complaint.

  • Customer: Excuse me.
  • Waiter: Yes. Can I help you?
  • Customer: I think there is a problem with my order. I asked for the fish and this is chicken.
  • Waiter: I am so sorry. I will go ask for a new meal right now. I apologize for the mistake.
  • Customer: Thank you

Do

Write a letter to the apartment manager, Mr. Lopez, and explain that there is a problem with your refrigerator. Tell what is wrong and give information about the problem.

Speak to a partner. Call your manager, Mr. Lopez, and complain about the refrigerator.

Listen to your partner. Pretend you are Mr. Lopez and respond to the complaint.

Read:

  • I think there is a problem with my order.
  • The customer ordered fish but received chicken.

Write:

  • Example: Dear Mr. Lopez, I am sorry, but I think there is a problem with my refrigerator. When I got home from work yesterday, my food was warm. I checked again this morning, and all of my food is bad. It is not cold. Can you help me with this? Thank you.

Speak:

  • Example: Hello, I am calling because there is something wrong with my refrigerator. Can you help me?

Listen:

  • Example: I am sorry to hear that. I will send someone to look at it.
