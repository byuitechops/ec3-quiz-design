Coordinate plans

CAN-DO: Can understand and coordinate appointments and plans

Learn

You will work with another person or with a group to coordinate plans. You will agree on important information such as who, what, when, and where. You can also tell about your wants and needs, assign responsibilities, and confirm plans.

Vocabulary

  • Coordinate: to get thing in order
  • Suggest: to introduce and idea
  • Preference: to like one thing more than another
  • Responsibility: to be in charge of
  • Confirm: to make sure

Study

State wants and needs and make suggestions.

  • We should meet for lunch
  • Let’s have a party.

Talk about who, what, when, where.

  • Let’s invite _____.
  • Let’s play some games.
  • We’ll need _____.
  • Let’s do it on _____.
  • It starts at _____.
  • It will last for about _____.
  • Where shall we go?

State preferences.

  • I prefer hamburgers.

Assign responsibilities

  • Can/Will/Would you bring the drinks?

Confirm plans

  • How does that sound?

Practice

Readthe conversation. Identify all of the plans.

  • A: Friday is Maria’s birthday. We should do something for her.
  • B: Yeah. Let’s have a party.
  • A: Good idea. We could take her out to eat.
  • B: Well, why don’t we get together at my house? I think Maria would enjoy that more.
  • A: Okay. When should we have it?
  • B: What about 7:00 Friday night?
  • A: That would be good.
  • B: Who should we invite?
  • A: How about Perla, Jose, Ricardo, Jon, Carla, Julia, Toni, Sally, and George.
  • B: Sounds good. I’ll call everyone. Do you want me to get a gift?
  • A: Yeah. If you can do that, I’ll make a cake and get some ice cream.
  • B: What else should I bring?
  • A: Could you bring a couple of board games?
  • B: Sure.
  • A: Perfect.

Writea text message to a friend. Coordinate lunch. Include the date, time, location, and your preferences.
