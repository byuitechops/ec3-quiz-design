Make a reservation

CAN-DO: Can understand and coordinate appointments and plans

Learn

Sometimes you need to make a reservation to hold a hotel room, get a plane or train ticket, schedule a taxi, or save a table at a restaurant. You need to prepare your travel information in advance including dates and times you will leave and arrive. You also need to know how many people are in your group or party and the ages of children. You should be prepared to explain any medical conditions or allergies. Reservations are mostly made online, but sometimes you need to call the company.

Vocabulary

Reserve: arrange for ahead of time

Book: buy a ticket, or make a reservation

Rate: the price

Party: a group

Check in: the process of showing your ticket or reservation when you arrive at a hotel or airport

Check out: the process of formally paying any expenses and leaving a hotel

Arrival: get to a place

Departure: the time a plane or train leaves

Study

Hotel

Hello, I would like a room on Saturday for two adults. What is your rate?

Flight (with travel agent)

Hello, I would like a flight to New York City next month for two people. I prefer a direct flight. I can give you the dates.

Taxi

Hello, I'm at the airport. Can you send a taxi?

Restaurant

Hello, I would like to make a dinner reservation for tomorrow at seven. There will be four in our group.

Practice

Read the conversation.

A: Hello, I would like to make a hotel reservation.

B: Okay, I am happy to help you. When will you arrive?

A: I will check in Friday and stay for one night. We have two adults and two children.

B: We have a room available with two beds. It's non-smoking. After taxes, your total will be $75.25. Would you like me to book the room?

A: Yes. Thank you. I will give you my credit card information.

B: Great. Check in is at 3:00 P.M. and check out is at 11:00 A.M. the next day.

Do

    Write a text. Use the hotel conversation information and write a text to your friend to tell him/her about your hotel reservation. Tell the date, check in times, and cost.

    Speak with a partner. Use the hotel reservation conversation and practice booking a room.

    Listen to your partner speak the hotel reservation conversation and answer questions about the hotel reservation.
