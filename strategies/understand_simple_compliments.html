COMPLIMENTS

GOAL: Understand simple compliments

Learn

When giving a compliment, we usually say something nice about a person or thing and then ask a question about it, such as where it was bought, or how it was made.


We usually reply to a compliment in one of two ways: accepting it or rejecting it. When accepting a compliment, we usually thank the person and then either explain something about the the thing or about ourselves or give a similar compliment back. When rejecting a compliment, we still thank the person, but then say something to deny the compliment.

Vocabulary

Compliment: to praise someone

Reject: not to take, to refuse

Accept: to take or get, to receive

Study

Give a Compliment:

I would like to compliment you on…

I think your dress is very nice.

I just love your cooking.

I really like your new phone.

Accept a Compliment:

Thank you. It’s so nice of you to say so.

Thank you. I’m glad you like it.

Thank you.

Thanks.

Practice

Speak to a friend or partner and compliment him or her on the following: hair, singing, or another clothing.


Listen to the compliment and accept or reject it.
