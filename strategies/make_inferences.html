Identify Opinions

GOAL: Identify Opinions

Learn

An opinion is a view of something not based on fact. It can express someone’s thoughts, feelings, or ideas. Opinions cannot be proven. Opinions can be argued. People have different opinions.


When you read or listen, it is helpful to know when someone is presenting a fact or presenting their opinion. It is also important when you speak or write to know when you are presenting information as a fact or just your opinion.

Vocabulary

Opinion: a view of something not based on fact

Fact: a statement based on truth

Prove: show the truth of something

Argue: give reasons to support a fact or opinion

Thought: an idea produced by thinking

Feeling: an emotional reaction

Idea: a thought or suggestion

Study

Opinions:

Strawberry ice cream tastes better than chocolate ice cream.

Soccer is boring.

A dog is the best animal.

Practice

Read to identify 2 opinions in the paragraph:

Did you know that more than 1 million kinds of animals are on Earth? For some people, protecting wild animals is not important. But I think it is one of the most important issues of our time. 16,306 kinds of animals are endangered right now. There are many reasons why we should protect the world’s wildlife. People rely on animals, insects, and plants to live. We all need wildlife. The most important thing you can do to help is to learn how to protect the wildlife in your area.

Possible Answers

Reason for calling: I'd like to make an appointment to see Dr. Smith. I'm having trouble with my shoulder.

Confirmation: We'll see you tomorrow at 3:00.
