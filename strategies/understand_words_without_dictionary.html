Learn Vocabulary in Context

GOAL: Understand words without a dictionary

Learn

You will find new words as you read. Good readers do not translate every new word they find. Focus on the words that you can understand. The words you can understand are context clues (hints) that will help you to figure out the meaning of the new word.


Context clues may appear within the same sentence as the word to which it refers, or may be in a sentence either before or after.

Vocabulary

Synonyms: words with the same meaning

Antonyms: words with opposite meaning

Definition: the exact meaning of a word

Examples: giving additional information to help explain what you are saying

Restate: to say something again, but in a different way

Comparison: talking about things that are the same.

Contrast: talking about things that are different

Study

Context clues to use to understand new words

  • Synonyms: Honest people are trustworthy.
  • Antonyms: Light, mpt darkness, is good for reading.
  • Definitions: Dogs are canines>
  • Examples: Metopolises, like New York and Chicago, have many people.
  • Restate: I am enthusiastic, or excited, about reading.
  • Comparisons: Making rice is easy, but making bread is complex.
