COMPLEX QUESTIONS

GOAL: Learn to make Statement Questions

Learn

English has many ways to ask questions. Some are simple and some are complicated.


One complicated question type is statement questions. Statement questions are formed the same way sentences are, but they have a question mark at the end in writing, and our voices go up at the end in speaking.


We use statement questions for two reasons:

  1. To show we are surprised. For example, if you bought a new car, and I was surprised, I would say “You bought a new car?”
  2. To show we don’t believe something. For example, if the team usually loses all the games, and you say the team won a game, I would say “They won the game?”

Form statement questions in this way:

    subject + verb + object?

You ate all the candy?

Vocabulary

Subject: person/thing creating the action in a sentence.

Verb: the action in a sentence.

Object: person/thing receiving the action in a sentence.

Study

He said he loves you?

We are going on vacation?

You bought a new house?

Practice

Talk with a friend. Ask your friend statement questions about his/her life.

Possible answers

- You studied all night?

- You are a famous singer?

- You play the piano and the violin?
