COMPARISON AND CONTRAST

GOAL: Learn ways to organize a comparison/contrast paper or presentation.

Learn

Compare means to find similarities between two or more people, ideas, events, or things. Contrast means to find differences between two or more people, ideas, events, or things.


A paper or presentation that compares/contrasts can be done two ways.

  1. Subject by subject
  2. Point by point

Study

You are comparing two individuals who applied for a job to determine who is the most qualified.


Subject by Subject Method:


Talk about one candidate and then the other. Use the same points of comparison for each.

Paolo

  • Interview - gave good examples
  • Work Experience - customer communications firm in Brazil; manager of a customer relations firm
  • Collegiality - easy going
  • Availability - can begin in 30 days

Mary

  • Interview - gave clear answers
  • Work experience - manager of a human resources department; marketing director in a retail business
  • Collegiality - friendly and open
  • Availability - can begin in 2 weeks

Point by Point Method:


Talk about each point of comparison for each candidate.

Interview
  • Paulo
  • Mary

Work experience

  • Paulo
  • Mary

Collegiality

  • Paulo
  • Mary

Availability

  • Paulo
  • Mary

Practice

Write a short paragraph comparing or contrasting two people, movies, phones, or a topic of your choice.


Decide on your points of comparison or contrast.
