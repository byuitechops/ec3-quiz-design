Understand simple yes/no questions while listening

CAN-DO: Can ask and answer yes/no questions

Learn

The answer to many of our questions is yes or no. These are called “yes/no questions.” We make yes/no questions in these ways:


Be + Subject + Object

Is he home? Yes, he is. No, he isn't.


Do/Does + Subject + Verb + Object

Does he like pizza? Yes, he does. No, he doesn't.)


Auxillary + Subject + Verb + Object

Can he eat pizza? Yes, he can. No, he can't.

Vocabulary

Subject: the person/thing making the action in a sentence

Verb: the action word in a sentence

Object: the person/thing that receives the action in a sentence

Auxillary: words that give us more information about the action in a sentence


Study

Mary: Hi John! Do you like pizza?

John: Hi Mary! No, I do not like pizza. Do you like pizza?

Mary: Yes, I do.

John: Are you happy when you eat pizza?

Mary: Yes, I am. Are you sad when you eat pizza?

John: No, I am not. But I don't like it.

Mary: Can you try pizza again?

John: Yes, I can. I will try it again for you.


Practice

Make a new friend. Ask your new friend about things he or she does/doesn’t like to do. Ask your new friend about things he or she is/isn’t. Ask your new friend about things he or she can/can’t do.


Examples:

Do you like cookies?

Can you paint?

Are you from here?
