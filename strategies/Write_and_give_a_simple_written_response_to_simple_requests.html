Respond to requests

GOAL: Write and give a simple written response to simple requests

Learn

Requests are questions used to ask others to do thing. Requests usually begin with one of the following word groups:Will you, Would you, Can you, Could you. Polite requests usually begin with would and could.


Answers to requests are either “yes” or no”, or words similar to yes or no. They often repeat word from the offer. Here are some examples:

Yes, I can

Yeah

Sure

No, I can't

Vocabulary

  • Request: the act of asking for something
  • Message: information that is given or sent to someone
  • Response: an answer

Study

Text Message

Ricardo: Hey Santiago, Can you help me move? We’ll be starting around 1:00.

Santiago: Yes, I can totally help out!

Ricardo: Great!


Email Request


Amanda,


I am planning a birthday party for Emily. Would you like to help with the food? We will have dinner and snacks. The party is next Friday. Please let me know soon.


Emma

Email Response

Emma,


Yes. I would love to help. Call me and we can plan for the food.


Amanda

Practice

Read the text message and find the response.

Tom: You won’t believe the day I’ve had.

Maria: What happened?

Tom: I was late to work and now my car won’t start. Could you come over and help me?

Maria: Yes. I can help you start your car. I’ll be there soon.

Tom: Thank you!
