MAKE APPOINTMENTS

GOAL: Understand and use information questions

Learn

When we need to get information from someone, we ask a question. Questions that have information as the answer are called WH questions, or information questions. For example,

  • How are you? (information answer: I am well.)
  • What time is it? (information answer: it is 3:00.)
  • Why are you laughing? (information answer: I heard a joke.)

Make information questions in these ways:


Information Questions:


If the verb is BE

WH word + be + object

When is your birthday?

To ask about the subject

WH word + main verb + object

Who eats pizza?

To ask about the subject

WH word + auxillary + subject + verb

Where do you work?

Vocabulary

Subject: the person/thing making the action in a sentence.

Verb: the action word in a sentence.

Object: the person/thing that receives the action in a sentence.

Auxillary: words that give us more information about the action in a sentence.


WH Words

  • Who: asks about a person
  • What: asks about things
  • Where: asks about location
  • When: asks about time
  • Why: asks about purpose
  • How: asks about the manner the action is done
