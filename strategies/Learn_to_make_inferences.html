Learn to make inferences

CAN-DO: Can understand and make inferences

Learn

Making an inference means you use the information you understand and know to make conclusions about information you don’t know.


For example, if your friend comes into class wearing a coat and carrying an umbrella, you can make an inference: it is probably cold and wet outside. You know this about the weather even if your friend does not tell you. You used the information you know (coat, umbrella) to make the conclusion that the weather is cold and wet. This is an inference.

Vocabulary

  • Inferences: a guess or conclusion, using information you understand and know, to help you understand information you don't know

Study

John: I eat pizza everyday. Pizza is delicious.

From this information, we can infer (guess/ conclude) that John's favorite food is pizza. He did not say it was his favorite food. But the things he said make it easy to guess that pizza is his favorite food.

Practice

Make inferences using this information:

Mary: I feel cold. I don’t have a jacket. John, is the window open? (Possible inference: Mary wants John to close the window.)


John: What are you doing tonight, Mary? My friends and I are going to a movie. (Possible inference: John wants Mary to come to the movie.)


Jane: David, the baby is crying and I am busy making dinner! (Possible inference: Jane wants David to go help the baby.)
